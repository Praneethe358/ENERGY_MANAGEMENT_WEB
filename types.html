Renewable Energy Resources

These resources are naturally replenished and are considered environmentally friendly.

Solar Energy

(PV) Cells: Convert sunlight directly into electricity.

Solar Thermal:

Uses sunlight to generate heat for electricity or heating.

Energy

Onshore Wind Power: Turbines placed on land. Offshore Wind Power: Turbines installed in bodies of water.

Hydropower (Water Energy)

Dams: Use the flow of water to generate electricity. Run-of-River: Uses river flow without large dams. Tidal and Wave Energy: Harnesses energy from tides and waves.

Biomass Energy

Wood, Agricultural Residues, and Waste: Burned or converted to biofuels. Biofuels: Ethanol, biodiesel, and biogas from organic materials.

geothermal Energy

Heat from the Earth's Interior: Used for electricity generation and direct heating.

Hydrogen

Green Hydrogen: Produced using renewable energy sources through electrolysis.
